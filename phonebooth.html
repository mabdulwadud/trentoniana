A Call From the Past

How to Interact

Below you will see a depiction of the phonebooth found in the Trentoniana Room. In its current state, it is run-down and dilapidated. Usually, there would be functioning accordion doors and a payphone hooked up on the interior that anyone could use.

Beside the phonebooth (or below if you're on mobile), there are clickable sound files that you can listen to. The voices you will hear are generated by Artificial Intelligence. This is an attempt to bring you as close as you can possibly get to hearing the calls that might have taken place.

Up until the early 70s, Trenton's downtown area had a handful of movie theaters. This would have included the RKO International 70, RKO State Theater, RKO Lincoln to name a few. Perhaps the most popular of them all was the RKO Lincoln N. Warren St. This theater opened April 24, 1928. It boasted over 2300 seats and the latest film titles. It was quite the sight for citygoers.
